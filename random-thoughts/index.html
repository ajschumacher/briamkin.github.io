NJ Gas Dilemma

So I was driving back to NY from New Jersey (the armpit state of the US and I remembered that I should fill up my gas tank because gas is way cheaper in NJ (about 50 cents/gal cheaper) and it is all still full service! As I'm driving down Route 46 I pass a gas station with a price of $2.31, not too bad. But maybe there's one that's cheaper, so I drive a little further and find one for $2.29. I think I can still do better than that, BUT I'm also getting closer to the bridge and when I reach the bridge to NY, there's no turning back without paying that insane $12 toll twice. So I turned in to fill my tank at $2.29. Luckily, there were no other gas stations! I made the right choice.

But, it got me thinking, is there a way for me to build a probability model to help me with that choice in the future? This also has applications to many other things like knowing when to buy plane tickets, or the best time to buy a stock, or if I should go back to school to get a Master's degree. How long should we keep waiting for a certain scenario to pan out before eating our potential losses?

The short answer is, I don't know. But the longer answer is, it might take me a while, but I'll find out! Whether it's using a mixture of risk analysis, opportunity cost analysis or even game theory, there's gotta be a way! Maybe I'll even consider a similar concept for my final project...
