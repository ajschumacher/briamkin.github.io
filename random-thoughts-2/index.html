Porsche Ads on Local Buses

I was driving around Van Cortlandt station earlier this week and noticed a bus with an ad for Porsches! I was a little confused because, from a market perspective, I couldn't figure out why Porsche would want to advertize their cars on a local bus in a not-so-nice area. As a luxury brand, don't they want to be associated with luxury, not a bus that usually has ads for community colleges?

The bus looks something like this:

So I thought about it for a while. I also saw more buses with ads for other luxury brands like Mercedes, like in the photo above.

Conclusion

I don't really have a conclusion but I did come up with a possible theory. The people that are more likely to be able to afford one might be in cars driving near the bus. Also, maybe if the ad is juxtaposed and contrasted with a "lower" class mode of transportation, it looks that much more luxurious? I really don't know if I buy that theory... Any thoughts?
